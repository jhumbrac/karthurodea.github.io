A Digital Resume

The traditional resume is boring. Instead I've chosen to build this timeline of my personal and work experiences. Starting from my early days growing up in Vermont; onto my four "developmental" years in Worcester, MA; culminating in the present day. Consider these my defining moments of each epoch.

1985 - 2002

I grew up in Arlington, Vermont, a town with just over 2300 people. Our claim to fame is an association with Norman Rockwell,who lived in Arlington from 1939-1953. Between the ages of 10 and 18 I spent all my time playing soccer, basketball, and baseball. I wish I could weave together a more interesting series of events, but as the seasons changed I transitioned from one sport to the next. To wit, peruse these old newspaper clippings that were carefully stored in a shoebox in my parents' attic until a few months ago.

2003-2007

I left Vermont for Holy Cross College in Massachusetts, which boasts a student body of 2900, a little larger than the population of my hometown. Toward the end of high school baseball had become my primary pursuit, and I found my way onto the college baseball team as a walk-on. Freshman year was lots of ups and downs, strikes and gutters; I struggled to balance coursework unlike anything I experienced in high school with the daily pain and discomfort of division I baseball (and a deep desire to learn every bar in Worcester).

After two years of dormitory living and dining hall cuisine (and a career-defining win at Harvard) I moved off campus with 7 baseball teammates and 2 friends-of-the-program to 125 College Street, where we spent our remaining two years staving off graduation, patronizing Wendy's drive-thru, and entertaining classmates. I love this picture because it is NOTHING like the condition of the house during my tenancy. I couldn't find a picture of the exterior, but do your best to imagine the worst, then add ten additional degrees of worst.

  • Skills Learned:
  • Critical Thinking;
  • Communication;
  • Collaboration

I graduated from Holy Cross with a degree in English, and spent the next five months living with my aunt and uncle in Chelmsford seeking employment. My first employer - Experian QAS - tapped my formal understanding of the English language, paying me to sell mailing address validation software to local, state, and federal government agencies in Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana, and Arkansas. After a couple months of gainful employment, I moved to Allston and rented an apartment with a college roommate. I consider this a transitional moment in my history, moving from the comfort and security of living with family, to the struggle of independence, and a paycheck-to-paycheck modus operandi.

  • Skills Learned:
  • Cold Calling;
  • Meeting Prep and Presentation;
  • Humility

Whilst employed at Experian I relocated from Allston to Charlestown, and shared an excellent two-bedroom with a new roommate (a friend of a friend). Shortly after the reloaction I parted ways with Experian and software sales, and got my first and last taste of contract staffing at Bowdoin Group. I started at BG in January 2009, and left BG in January 2010.

  • Skills Learned:
  • Persistence;
  • Resilience;
  • Time Management

2008 - Present

When I left the Bowdoin Group I was concerned the last year had been a waste. However, the time I spent cold-calling and pushing past frequent rejection became a valuable asset. I worked my way through the interview process at Forrester Research and received an offer to work in their account management department. One of the people on the interview panel at Forrester came from the staffing world, and I think my experience at the Bowdoin Group was a big driver in the decision to offer me the job. The role was supporting two senior account directors and the ~20 enterprise clients they managed.
