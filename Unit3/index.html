I grew up in Arlington, Vermont, a town with just over 2300 people. Our claim to fame is an association with Norman Rockwell,who lived in Arlington from 1939-1953.

Between the ages of 10 and 18 I spent all my time playing soccer, basketball, and baseball. I wish I could weave together a more interesting series of events; but the truth is as the seasons changed I went from one sport to the next ... competing - obviously - with the country's best young talent in Vermont's hotbed of youth sports. To wit, peruse these old newspaper clippings that were carefully stored in a shoebox in my parents' attic until a few months ago.

I left Vermont for Holy Cross College in Massachusetts, which boasts a student body of 2900, a little larger than the population of my hometown. Toward the end of high school baseball had become my primary pursuit, and I found my way onto the college baseball team as a walk-on.

After two years of dormitory living and several trips to the school's disciplinary office I moved off campus with 7 baseball teammates and 2 friends-of-the-program to 125 College Streetwhere we spent our remaining two years staving off graduation and entertaining classmates.
